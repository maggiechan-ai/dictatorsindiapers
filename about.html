About The Movement

We're building something that doesn't exist yet: the world's largest community specifically for dads of kids aged 0–5.

Why This Exists

Here's what happens when you become a dad:

The pediatrician talks to your wife. The parenting books are written for moms. The baby groups at the library? Full of moms. The "parent" influencers on Instagram? Mostly moms. The school pickup text chain? Moms.

And look — that's not a complaint about moms. Moms are incredible. Moms have built amazing communities. But somewhere along the way, dads got left out of the conversation.

Not because we don't care. Because the infrastructure wasn't built for us.

So we're building it.

Dictators in Diapers is a community for dads in the trenches of raising kids aged 0–5. The sleepless nights. The toddler negotiations. The meltdowns (theirs and ours). The identity crisis. The joy that hits you at random moments when your kid does something that makes your heart explode.

We call them dictators because — let's be honest — that's what they are. Tiny, irrational leaders who control every aspect of your life and somehow you'd die for them without hesitation.

This isn't a parenting advice site. It's not a medical resource. It's dads talking to dads. Honest, funny, sometimes raw, always real.

What We Believe

Dads need their own space.

Not because we can't be in "parent" spaces. Because sometimes you need to talk to someone who gets exactly what you're going through.

Funny and serious aren't opposites.

We can laugh about the chaos AND talk about postpartum depression in dads. Humor is how we cope. Honesty is how we heal.

Every dad's story matters.

Single dads. Stay-at-home dads. Working-three-jobs dads. First-time dads. Fifth-time dads. All of you are welcome here.

Vulnerability is strength.

The bravest thing a dad can do is say "I'm struggling" out loud. We're building a space where that's not just accepted — it's celebrated.
