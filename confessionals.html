Dad Confessionals

Anonymous. Honest. The stuff dads actually think but never say out loud.

I told my wife I was "working late" but I was actually sitting in the car in the driveway for 20 minutes listening to a podcast before going inside. The toddler had been screaming since 5am. I just needed silence. I'm not proud. But I'm not sorry.
— Anonymous Dad, 2 kids, ages 1 & 3
My 3-year-old asked me where babies come from. I panicked and said "Amazon." My wife overheard and hasn't stopped laughing at me for three days. The kid now thinks Prime delivery includes siblings.
— Anonymous Dad, 1 kid, age 3
I have perfected the art of pretending to be asleep when the baby cries at 3am. I know my wife knows. She knows I know she knows. We've never discussed it. This is marriage.
— Anonymous Dad, 1 kid, age 8 months
I cried in the shower after my son's first day of preschool. Not because I was sad. Because I had four uninterrupted hours for the first time in three years and I didn't know what to do with myself.
— Anonymous Dad, 1 kid, age 3
My daughter's favorite word is "no." Her second favorite word is "mine." Her third favorite word is "daddy" — but only when she wants something. I have never felt more used and more loved at the same time.
— Anonymous Dad, 2 kids, ages 2 & 4
I Googled "is it normal to not feel bonded with your newborn" at 2am in the hospital. Every article said "give it time." They were right. But nobody told me how terrifying those first weeks would be when you feel nothing and think something's wrong with you.
— Anonymous Dad, 1 kid, age 14 months
The highlight of my week is grocery shopping alone. Not because I love groceries. Because for 45 minutes, nobody is touching me, asking me for snacks, or having an emotional breakdown about sock texture.
— Anonymous Dad, 3 kids, ages 1, 3, & 5
My 4-year-old told her preschool teacher that "daddy says bad words when he builds IKEA furniture." She's not wrong. I'm not even mad. I'm impressed by her reporting accuracy.
— Anonymous Dad, 1 kid, age 4
